AI Literacy Paths for TCM

Leading the Future of Integrative Medicine

This initiative provides a strategic framework to ensure every TCM professional can become 'bilingual'—fluent in the principles of Traditional Chinese Medicine and in the application of AI to advance their practice, teaching, and research.

Guiding Principles

This framework is built on two core philosophies that prioritize human intellect and ethical integrity in the age of AI.

Clarity of Thought

Effective AI collaboration is not about "prompting tricks"; it is a reflection of our own thinking. We emphasize a Critical Thinking Framework—meta-clarity, systematic deconstruction, and iterative refinement—to use AI as a tool to sharpen our own intellect, not replace it.

Meaningful Human Control

In high-stakes fields like medicine, the human must remain the ultimate clinical reasoner. We are guided by the principles of Explainable AI (XAI), advocating for systems that allow practitioners to understand, challenge, and take full responsibility for AI-assisted decisions, thereby preserving their epistemological integrity.

Starting Point

Core Curriculum for All Paths

Every journey begins with a shared foundation, ensuring a common language and ethical grounding in the principles of artificial intelligence.

Module 1: AI Foundations

Objective: To build a functional understanding of core AI technologies and their current applications within healthcare and TCM.

  • L1Differentiate between AI, Machine Learning, and LLMs.
  • L1Apply the Critical Thinking Framework to a simple query.

Module 2: Responsible AI

Objective: To instill a deep understanding of the ethical responsibilities associated with using AI in a clinical and academic setting.

  • L1Articulate the principles of Meaningful Human Control.
  • L2Identify potential ethical risks in a provided case study.
  • L3Analyze a diagnostic AI tool for potential bias and lack of explainability.
Specializations

Role-Based Learning Paths

After the core curriculum, learners choose a path that aligns with their professional goals, turning foundational knowledge into specialized, actionable skills.

Practitioner Path

Focus: Augmenting clinical intelligence while maintaining practitioner-led care.

AI as Reasoning Partner

  • L2Use an AI tool to generate differential diagnoses.
  • L3Critique AI outputs for clinical accuracy and epistemological soundness.

Intelligent Practice Mgmt.

  • L2Develop a "human-in-the-loop" workflow for an AI scribe.
  • L3Design a protocol for an AI-assisted telehealth triage system.

Educator Path

AI-Infused Course Design

  • L2Teach the Critical Thinking Framework to students.
  • L3Design an assignment that requires ethical AI use and reflection.

Pedagogy for MHC

  • L3Create a course AI policy that promotes Meaningful Human Control.
  • L4Develop a new assessment model that evaluates human-AI collaboration.

Student Path

Focus: Building a competitive edge to become a future-ready, critically-minded TCM innovator.

AI-Powered Research

  • L2Apply the Critical Thinking Framework to a literature review.
  • L3Evaluate the credibility and potential bias of AI-generated summaries.

Innovation & Prototyping

  • L3Analyze the ethical implications of a proposed AI-in-TCM tool.
  • L4Draft a conceptual proposal for a new tool that adheres to MHC principles.

AI Literacy Levels

The competencies throughout this framework are organized into four distinct levels of cognitive skill.

L1Understand
Recalling facts and basic concepts.
L2Apply
Using concepts and methods in new situations.
L3Analyze & Evaluate
Drawing connections, justifying a stance or decision.
L4Create
Producing new or original work.

Levels adapted from Bloom's Taxonomy and the Framework for AI Literacy | EDUCAUSE Review (June 3, 2024).
